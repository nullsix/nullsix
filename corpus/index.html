corpus // text analysis

Every chart below is computed straight from the source text: Thoughts of an Eaten Sun (a grimdark fantasy novel), Genny (a near-future novella), and the published essays of my old blog, Sundered Peak. Word counts, sentence rhythm, character presence, and the made-up words left over when you subtract the English.

Two books, four measures

Each measure over normalized book progress, so an 81K-word novel and a 23K-word novella can share an axis.

Each book's language

Log-odds over the two vocabularies: the words that most mark each book as itself.

Invented words

Subtract every word English knows (plus its inflections) and this is the residue: the coined vocabulary of each book, separated from proper names. Hover a word for where it first appears.

Built from the raw manuscript markdown with a small Python pipeline (word/sentence segmentation, MATTR, hapax rate, Flesch, log-odds distinctive vocabulary, gazetteer-matched character presence, and an English-subtraction pass via wordfreq). Charts are hand-rolled SVG — no libraries, no tracking, nothing loaded from anywhere.
